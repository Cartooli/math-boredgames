üìö What Are Ratios?

A ratio compares two quantities. Written as a:b or a/b

Example: 3 red marbles to 5 blue marbles
Ratio = 3:5 or 3/5 or "3 to 5"

üìä Rates

A rate is a ratio comparing different units (like miles per hour)

Examples:
60 miles per 1 hour = 60 mph
$3 per 2 apples = $1.50 per apple

‚öñÔ∏è Proportions

A proportion is an equation showing two ratios are equal: a/b = c/d

Cross-Multiplication:
If 2/3 = x/6, then 2√ó6 = 3√óx
12 = 3x ‚Üí x = 4

üí° Example

Example: Proportion Problem

Problem: If 3 pencils cost $2, how much do 9 pencils cost?

Solution: 3/2 = 9/x ‚Üí Cross-multiply: 3x = 18 ‚Üí x = $6

‚úèÔ∏è Practice

Problem 1: Write the ratio of 4 cats to 7 dogs

Answer: 4:7 or 4/7

Problem 2: Solve: 2/5 = x/15

Hint: Cross-multiply: 2√ó15 = 5√óx
Answer: x = 6 (30 = 5x, so x = 6)

Problem 3: A car goes 120 miles in 2 hours. What's the rate in mph?

Answer: 60 mph (120√∑2)
