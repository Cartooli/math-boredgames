Division and Fair Sharing

Division is splitting a total into equal groups. When you share 12 cookies equally among 3 friends, each friend gets 4 cookies. That is division! Division is the inverse (opposite) of multiplication, just like subtraction is the inverse of addition.

Two Ways to Think About Division

Division answers two kinds of questions:

Fair Sharing (Partitive)

"12 cookies shared equally among 3 friends. How many does each friend get?"

12 ÷ 3 = 4

Making Groups (Measurement)

"I have 12 cookies and want to put 4 in each bag. How many bags do I need?"

12 ÷ 4 = 3

Both questions use the same numbers (12, 3, 4), just asked differently.

Division and Multiplication Are Inverses

If you know a multiplication fact, you already know a division fact!

From Multiplication to Division

If you know:

6 x 4 = 24

Then you also know:

24 ÷ 6 = 4   and   24 ÷ 4 = 6

To solve a division problem, ask: "What times the divisor equals the dividend?"

Using Multiplication to Divide: 35 ÷ 5

Ask yourself: "5 times what equals 35?"

5 x ? = 35

Think through the 5s table: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30, 35. That is the 7th multiple.

So 35 ÷ 5 = 7.

Division with Remainders

Sometimes things do not divide evenly. If you share 14 stickers among 3 friends, each gets 4 stickers (3 x 4 = 12), but 2 stickers are left over. We call those leftovers the remainder.

Division with a Remainder: 14 ÷ 3

  1. What is the biggest multiple of 3 that fits in 14? Try: 3 x 4 = 12 (fits!), 3 x 5 = 15 (too big!).
  2. Each person gets 4.
  3. Used: 3 x 4 = 12. Leftover: 14 - 12 = 2.
14 ÷ 3 = 4 remainder 2

Common Mistake

Students sometimes mix up the dividend and divisor. In 12 ÷ 3, the 12 (dividend) is what you are splitting up, and the 3 (divisor) is how many groups you make (or the size of each group). The answer (4) is the quotient.

Helpful Tip

To check any division answer, multiply: quotient x divisor + remainder = dividend. For 14 ÷ 3 = 4 R 2: check 4 x 3 + 2 = 12 + 2 = 14. Correct!

Practice Problems

1. 20 ÷ 4 = ?

Show Solution

Think: 4 x ? = 20. Since 4 x 5 = 20, the answer is 5.

2. 36 ÷ 6 = ?

Show Solution

Think: 6 x ? = 36. Since 6 x 6 = 36, the answer is 6.

3. 17 ÷ 5 = ? (include the remainder)

Show Solution

5 x 3 = 15 (fits), 5 x 4 = 20 (too big). So the quotient is 3, and the remainder is 17 - 15 = 2. Answer: 3 remainder 2. Check: 3 x 5 + 2 = 17.

4. 48 students need to form teams of 8. How many teams?

Show Solution

48 ÷ 8 = 6 teams. Check: 6 x 8 = 48.

5. Write the fact family for 7, 8, and 56.

Show Solution

7 x 8 = 56
8 x 7 = 56
56 ÷ 7 = 8
56 ÷ 8 = 7

Summary: Division splits a total into equal groups. It is the inverse of multiplication -- if you know your times tables, you can divide! When items do not split evenly, there is a remainder. Always check division by multiplying back.
