You have 12 cookies and want to share them equally among 3 friends. How many cookies does each friend get? When we split things into equal groups or share fairly, we use division! Division is the opposite of multiplication‚Äîthey work together like partners.

üìö What Is Division?

Division means splitting a number into equal groups. We use the division sign (√∑) or sometimes a slash (/).

üìä Division in Action

üçïüçïüçïüçïüçïüçïüçïüçï √∑ 4 = 2 slices per person

8 pizzas √∑ 4 people = 2 slices each

We read this as: "Eight divided by four equals two"

Two Ways to Think About Division

1. Fair Sharing: You know how many groups, find how many in each
Example: 12 candies √∑ 3 friends = ? candies each
Answer: 4 candies per friend

2. Making Groups: You know how many in each group, find how many groups
Example: 12 candies, 4 per bag = ? bags
Answer: 3 bags

Division and Multiplication: Partners!

Just like addition and subtraction, division and multiplication are inverse operations!

If you know: 3 √ó 4 = 12
Then you also know:
‚Ä¢ 12 √∑ 3 = 4
‚Ä¢ 12 √∑ 4 = 3

They're all part of the same fact family!

‚úì Pro Tip: To check your division, multiply the answer by the divisor! If 20 √∑ 4 = 5, then 5 √ó 4 should equal 20. ‚úì

Parts of a Division Problem

15 √∑ 3 = 5

15 = Dividend (what you're dividing)
√∑ = Division sign
3 = Divisor (what you're dividing by)
5 = Quotient (the answer)

üí° Let's Practice Division!

Example 1: Fair Sharing

Problem: You have 10 apples to share equally among 2 friends. How many apples does each friend get?

Step 1: Write as division ‚Üí 10 √∑ 2 = ?
Step 2: Give 1 apple to each friend, then another, and another...
Step 3: After 5 rounds, all 10 apples are shared

Answer: Each friend gets 5 apples

Example 2: Using Multiplication

Problem: What is 18 √∑ 6?

Step 1: Think of it as multiplication
Step 2: Ask: "6 √ó ? = 18"
Step 3: You know that 6 √ó 3 = 18
Step 4: So 18 √∑ 6 = 3

Answer: 18 √∑ 6 = 3

üí° Key Insight: Knowing your multiplication facts makes division easy!

Example 3: Real-World Division

Problem: A teacher has 24 pencils. She wants to put them in boxes with 6 pencils in each box. How many boxes does she need?

Step 1: 24 pencils total, 6 in each box
Step 2: Write it ‚Üí 24 √∑ 6 = ?
Step 3: Think: 6 √ó ? = 24
Step 4: 6 √ó 4 = 24, so 24 √∑ 6 = 4

Answer: She needs 4 boxes

üéØ Key Takeaways

  • Division means splitting into equal groups or fair sharing
  • Division and multiplication are inverse operations
  • Use multiplication to solve division: "? √ó divisor = dividend"
  • Check your work by multiplying the quotient times the divisor
  • Division answers the question "how many in each?" or "how many groups?"
