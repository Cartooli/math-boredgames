üìö The Foundation: Right Triangles

Trigonometry literally means "triangle measurement." For now, we focus on right triangles‚Äîtriangles with one 90¬∞ angle. Every right triangle has three sides with special names relative to a particular angle:

üìä Right Triangle Anatomy

                |\
                | \  ‚Üê Hypotenuse (longest side, opposite the right angle)
                |  \
                |   \
                |____\
            Opposite   Adjacent
            (to angle)  (to angle)

Hypotenuse: Always the longest side, opposite the 90¬∞ angle
Opposite: The side across from the angle you're focusing on
Adjacent: The side next to the angle (not the hypotenuse)

üéØ The Three Trig Ratios

Here's the key insight: In similar right triangles, the ratios of side lengths stay constant! These ratios have names: sine, cosine, and tangent.

SOH-CAH-TOA

Sine = Opposite / Hypotenuse
sin(Œ∏) = Opposite / Hypotenuse

Cosine = Adjacent / Hypotenuse
cos(Œ∏) = Adjacent / Hypotenuse

Tangent = Opposite / Adjacent
tan(Œ∏) = Opposite / Adjacent

Œ∏ (theta) represents the angle we're measuring from

‚úì Memory Tip: Think of SOH-CAH-TOA as a made-up name: "So-kuh-toe-uh." Say it a few times and you'll never forget the trig ratios!

When to Use Each Ratio

Choose your ratio based on what information you have:

Use SINE when you have (or want) opposite and hypotenuse
Use COSINE when you have (or want) adjacent and hypotenuse
Use TANGENT when you have (or want) opposite and adjacent
