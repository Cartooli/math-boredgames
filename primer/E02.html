‚ûï Let's Add Things Together!

You have 2 toy cars. Your friend gives you 3 more cars. How many do you have now? When we put groups together to find out "how many in all," we're using addition! Addition is one of the most useful things you'll learn in math.

üìö What Is Addition?

Addition means combining two or more groups to find the total. We use the plus sign (+) to show addition, and the equals sign (=) to show the answer.

üìä Addition in Action

2 + 3 = 5

We read this as: "Two plus three equals five" or "Two and three makes five"

Parts of an Addition Problem

3 + 5 = 8

3 = First addend (first number)
+ = Plus sign (add)
5 = Second addend (second number)
= = Equals sign
8 = Sum (the total/answer)

Smart Addition Strategies

Here are tricks to make addition easier:

1. Count On: Start with the bigger number, then count up
Example: 7 + 2 ‚Üí Start at 7, count: "8, 9" ‚Üí Answer: 9

2. Make 10: Look for ways to make 10 first
Example: 8 + 5 ‚Üí (8 + 2) + 3 ‚Üí 10 + 3 = 13

3. Use Doubles: Numbers added to themselves are easy!
3+3=6, 4+4=8, 5+5=10
‚úì Pro Tip: Addition can be done in any order! 3 + 5 gives the same answer as 5 + 3. This is called the "Commutative Property" (but you can just remember: order doesn't matter!)

üí° Let's Practice Adding!

Example 1: Simple Addition

Problem: You have 4 crayons. You get 3 more. How many crayons do you have?

Step 1: Write it as addition ‚Üí 4 + 3 = ?
Step 2: Count on from 4 ‚Üí "5, 6, 7"
Step 3: The answer is 7

Answer: You have 7 crayons

Example 2: Using the Make 10 Strategy

Problem: What is 9 + 4?

Step 1: 9 is close to 10, so let's make 10 first
Step 2: Take 1 from the 4 to add to 9 ‚Üí 9 + 1 = 10
Step 3: We have 3 left from the 4 ‚Üí 10 + 3 = 13

Answer: 9 + 4 = 13

üí° Key Insight: Making 10 first makes addition much easier!

Example 3: Real-World Addition

Problem: There are 5 birds on a tree. 6 more birds join them. How many birds are there now?

Step 1: Write the problem ‚Üí 5 + 6 = ?
Step 2: Use doubles strategy! 5 + 5 = 10
Step 3: Add one more ‚Üí 10 + 1 = 11

Answer: There are 11 birds on the tree

üéØ Key Takeaways

  • Addition means putting groups together to find the total
  • We use the + sign for addition and = sign for the answer
  • Count on from the bigger number to add quickly
  • Making 10 first makes addition easier
  • Order doesn't matter: 3 + 5 = 5 + 3
