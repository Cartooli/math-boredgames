üçï Fractions: Parts of a Whole!

Imagine cutting a pizza into slices. Each slice is a part of the whole pizza! When we talk about parts of a whole, we use fractions. Fractions help us share fairly, measure precisely, and understand parts of things in the world around us.

üìö What Are Fractions?

A fraction represents part of a whole. The whole is divided into equal parts, and the fraction tells us how many of those parts we have.

üìä Parts of a Fraction

3

4

3 = Numerator (how many parts we have)

We read this as: "three-fourths" or "three out of four parts"

üí° Let's Explore Fractions!

Example 1: Understanding Halves

Problem: You have a sandwich. You cut it into 2 equal parts and eat 1 part. What fraction did you eat?

Step 1: Count total equal parts ‚Üí 2 parts
Step 2: Count how many you have ‚Üí 1 part
Step 3: Write as a fraction ‚Üí 1/2

Answer: You ate 1/2 (one half) of the sandwich

Example 2: Thirds in Action

Problem: A pie is cut into 3 equal slices. You eat 2 slices. What fraction did you eat?

Step 1: Total equal parts ‚Üí 3
Step 2: Parts you ate ‚Üí 2
Step 3: Write it ‚Üí 2/3 (two-thirds)

Answer: You ate 2/3 of the pie

Example 3: Fourths/Quarters

Problem: A chocolate bar has 4 equal squares. You give away 3 squares. What fraction do you have left?

Step 1: Total squares ‚Üí 4
Step 2: Squares you have left ‚Üí 1
Step 3: Write it ‚Üí 1/4 (one-fourth or one-quarter)

Answer: You have 1/4 left

‚úèÔ∏è Your Turn with Fractions!

Problem 1: A circle is divided into 2 equal parts. 1 part is shaded. What fraction is shaded?

Hint: How many parts shaded / How many total parts
Answer: 1/2 (one half)

Problem 2: An orange has 4 equal slices. You eat 3 slices. What fraction did you eat?

Hint: 3 slices eaten out of 4 total
Answer: 3/4 (three-fourths)

Problem 3: A pizza is cut into 3 equal slices. You ate 1 slice. What fraction is left?

Hint: If 1 is eaten, how many of the 3 slices are left?
Answer: 2/3 (two-thirds) is left

Problem 4: Which is bigger: 1/2 or 1/4?

Hint: Think about pizza: would you rather have half a pizza or a quarter of a pizza?
Answer: 1/2 is bigger than 1/4

Problem 5: True or False: In fractions, all parts must be equal in size.

Hint: Remember the key rule!
Answer: TRUE! Equal parts are essential for fractions.

üéØ Key Takeaways

  • A fraction represents part of a whole
  • Numerator (top) = how many parts we have
  • Denominator (bottom) = total equal parts
  • All parts must be equal in size
  • Common fractions: 1/2 (half), 1/3 (third), 1/4 (fourth)
