üìö Equivalent Fractions

Equivalent fractions are fractions that represent the same value, even though they look different.

Example: 1/2 = 2/4 = 3/6 = 4/8

To create equivalent fractions:
Multiply (or divide) both numerator and denominator by the same number

1/2 √ó (2/2) = 2/4 ‚úì

üîç Simplifying Fractions

Simplifying means reducing a fraction to its simplest form (lowest terms). Divide both parts by their GCF.

Example: Simplify 6/8
1. Find GCF of 6 and 8 ‚Üí GCF = 2
2. Divide both by 2 ‚Üí 6√∑2 = 3, 8√∑2 = 4
3. Simplified: 3/4

üéØ Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

The GCF is the largest number that divides evenly into both numbers.

Finding GCF of 12 and 18:
Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12
Factors of 18: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18
Common: 1, 2, 3, 6
GCF = 6 (largest common factor)
